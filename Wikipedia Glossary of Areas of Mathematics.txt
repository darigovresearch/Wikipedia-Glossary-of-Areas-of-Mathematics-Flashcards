<b><a href=""/wiki/Absolute_geometry"" title=""Absolute geometry"">Absolute geometry</a></b>|an extension of <a href=""/wiki/Ordered_geometry"" title=""Ordered geometry"">ordered geometry</a> that is sometimes referred to as <i>neutral geometry</i> because its <a href=""/wiki/Axiom_system"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Axiom system"">axiom system</a> is neutral to the <a href=""/wiki/Parallel_postulate"" title=""Parallel postulate"">parallel postulate</a>.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Abstract_algebra"" title=""Abstract algebra"">Abstract algebra</a></b>|the study of <a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_structures"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Algebraic structures"">algebraic structures</a> and their properties. Originally it was known as <i>modern algebra</i>.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Abstract_analytic_number_theory"" title=""Abstract analytic number theory"">Abstract analytic number theory</a></b>|a branch of mathematics that takes ideas from <a href=""/wiki/Analytic_number_theory"" title=""Analytic number theory"">classical analytic number theory</a> and applies them to various other areas of mathematics.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Abstract_differential_geometry"" title=""Abstract differential geometry"">Abstract differential geometry</a></b>|a form of <a href=""/wiki/Differential_geometry"" title=""Differential geometry"">differential geometry</a> without the notion of <a href=""/wiki/Smoothness"" title=""Smoothness"">smoothness</a> from <a href=""/wiki/Calculus"" title=""Calculus"">calculus</a>. <b><a href=""/wiki/Abstract_harmonic_analysis"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Abstract harmonic analysis"">Abstract harmonic analysis</a></b>|a modern branch of <a href=""/wiki/Harmonic_analysis"" title=""Harmonic analysis"">harmonic analysis</a> that extends upon the generalized <a href=""/wiki/Fourier_transforms"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Fourier transforms"">Fourier transforms</a> that can be defined on <a href=""/wiki/Locally_compact_group"" title=""Locally compact group"">locally compact groups</a>.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Homotopy_theory"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Homotopy theory"">Abstract homotopy theory</a></b>|a part of <a href=""/wiki/Topology"" title=""Topology"">topology</a> that deals with homotopic functions, i.e. functions from one topological space to another which are homotopic (the functions can be deformed into one another).
<b><a href=""/wiki/Actuarial_science"" title=""Actuarial science"">Actuarial science</a></b>|the discipline that applies <a href=""/wiki/Mathematics"" title=""Mathematics"">mathematical</a> and <a href=""/wiki/Statistics"" title=""Statistics"">statistical</a> methods to <a href=""/wiki/Risk_assessment"" title=""Risk assessment"">assess risk</a> in <a href=""/wiki/Insurance"" title=""Insurance"">insurance</a>, <a href=""/wiki/Finance"" title=""Finance"">finance</a> and other industries and professions. More generally, actuaries apply rigorous mathematics to model matters of uncertainty.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Additive_combinatorics"" title=""Additive combinatorics"">Additive combinatorics</a></b>|the part of <a href=""/wiki/Arithmetic_combinatorics"" title=""Arithmetic combinatorics"">arithmetic combinatorics</a> devoted to the operations of <a href=""/wiki/Addition"" title=""Addition"">addition</a> and <a href=""/wiki/Subtraction"" title=""Subtraction"">subtraction</a>.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Additive_number_theory"" title=""Additive number theory"">Additive number theory</a></b>|a part of <a href=""/wiki/Number_theory"" title=""Number theory"">number theory</a> that studies subsets of <a href=""/wiki/Integer"" title=""Integer"">integers</a> and their behaviour under addition.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Affine_geometry"" title=""Affine geometry"">Affine geometry</a></b>|a branch of <a href=""/wiki/Geometry"" title=""Geometry"">geometry</a> that is centered on the study of geometric properties that remain unchanged by <a href=""/wiki/Affine_transformation"" title=""Affine transformation"">affine transformations</a>. It can be described as a generalization of Euclidean geometry.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Affine_geometry_of_curves"" title=""Affine geometry of curves"">Affine geometry of curves</a></b>|the study of <a href=""/wiki/Curve"" title=""Curve"">curves</a> in <a href=""/wiki/Affine_space"" title=""Affine space"">affine space</a>.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Affine_differential_geometry"" title=""Affine differential geometry"">Affine differential geometry</a></b>|a type of <a href=""/wiki/Differential_geometry"" title=""Differential geometry"">differential geometry</a> dedicated to differential <a href=""/wiki/Invariant_(mathematics)"" title=""Invariant (mathematics)"">invariants</a> under <a href=""/wiki/Volume"" title=""Volume"">volume</a>-preserving <a href=""/wiki/Affine_transformation"" title=""Affine transformation"">affine transformations</a>.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Ahlfors_theory"" title=""Ahlfors theory"">Ahlfors theory</a></b>|a part of <a href=""/wiki/Complex_analysis"" title=""Complex analysis"">complex analysis</a> being the geometric counterpart of <a href=""/wiki/Nevanlinna_theory"" title=""Nevanlinna theory"">Nevanlinna theory</a>. It was invented by <a href=""/wiki/Lars_Ahlfors"" title=""Lars Ahlfors"">Lars Ahlfors</a>
<b><a href=""/wiki/Algebra"" title=""Algebra"">Algebra</a></b>|a major part of <a href=""/wiki/Pure_mathematics"" title=""Pure mathematics"">pure mathematics</a> centered on <a href=""/wiki/Operation_(mathematics)"" title=""Operation (mathematics)"">operations</a> and <a href=""/wiki/Relation_(mathematics)"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Relation (mathematics)"">relations</a>. Beginning with <a href=""/wiki/Elementary_algebra"" title=""Elementary algebra"">elementary algebra</a>, it introduces the concept of <a href=""/wiki/Variable_(mathematics)"" title=""Variable (mathematics)"">variables</a> and how these can be manipulated towards <a href=""/wiki/Problem_solving"" title=""Problem solving"">problem solving</a>; known as <a href=""/wiki/Equation_solving"" title=""Equation solving"">equation solving</a>. Generalizations of <a href=""/wiki/Operation_(mathematics)"" title=""Operation (mathematics)"">operations</a> and <a href=""/wiki/Relation_(mathematics)"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Relation (mathematics)"">relations</a> defined on <a href=""/wiki/Set_(mathematics)"" title=""Set (mathematics)"">sets</a> have led to the idea of an <a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_structure"" title=""Algebraic structure"">algebraic structure</a> which are studied in abstract algebra. Other branches of algebra include <a href=""/wiki/Universal_algebra"" title=""Universal algebra"">universal algebra</a>, linear algebra and <a href=""/wiki/Multilinear_algebra"" title=""Multilinear algebra"">multilinear algebra</a>.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_analysis"" title=""Algebraic analysis"">Algebraic analysis</a></b>|motivated by systems of <a href=""/wiki/Linear_differential_equation"" title=""Linear differential equation"">linear</a> <a href=""/wiki/Partial_differential_equation"" title=""Partial differential equation"">partial differential equations</a>, it is a branch of <a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_geometry"" title=""Algebraic geometry"">algebraic geometry</a> and <a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_topology"" title=""Algebraic topology"">algebraic topology</a> that uses methods from <a href=""/wiki/Sheaf_theory"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Sheaf theory"">sheaf theory</a> and complex analysis, to study the properties and generalizations of <a href=""/wiki/Function_(mathematics)"" title=""Function (mathematics)"">functions</a>. It was started by <a href=""/wiki/Mikio_Sato"" title=""Mikio Sato"">Mikio Sato</a>.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_combinatorics"" title=""Algebraic combinatorics"">Algebraic combinatorics</a></b>|an area that employs methods of abstract algebra to problems of <a href=""/wiki/Combinatorics"" title=""Combinatorics"">combinatorics</a>. It also refers to the application of methods from combinatorics to problems in abstract algebra.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_computation"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Algebraic computation"">Algebraic computation</a></b>|see <i>symbolic computation</i>.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_geometry"" title=""Algebraic geometry"">Algebraic geometry</a></b>|a branch that combines techniques from abstract algebra with the language and problems of geometry. <b><a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_graph_theory"" title=""Algebraic graph theory"">Algebraic graph theory</a></b>|a branch of <a href=""/wiki/Graph_theory"" title=""Graph theory"">graph theory</a> in which methods are taken from algebra and employed to problems about <a href=""/wiki/Graph_(discrete_mathematics)"" title=""Graph (discrete mathematics)"">graphs</a>. The methods are commonly taken from <a href=""/wiki/Group_theory"" title=""Group theory"">group theory</a> and linear algebra.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_K-theory"" title=""Algebraic K-theory"">Algebraic K-theory</a></b>|an important part of <a href=""/wiki/Homological_algebra"" title=""Homological algebra"">homological algebra</a> concerned with defining and applying a certain sequence of <a href=""/wiki/Functor"" title=""Functor"">functors</a> from <a href=""/wiki/Ring_(mathematics)"" title=""Ring (mathematics)"">rings</a> to <a href=""/wiki/Abelian_group"" title=""Abelian group"">abelian groups</a>.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_number_theory"" title=""Algebraic number theory"">Algebraic number theory</a></b>|a part of algebraic geometry devoted to the study of the points of the <a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_varieties"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Algebraic varieties"">algebraic varieties</a> whose coordinates belong to an <a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_number_field"" title=""Algebraic number field"">algebraic number field</a>. It is a major branch of <a href=""/wiki/Number_theory"" title=""Number theory"">number theory</a> and is also said to study algebraic structures related to <a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_integer"" title=""Algebraic integer"">algebraic integers</a>.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_statistics"" title=""Algebraic statistics"">Algebraic statistics</a></b>|the use of algebra to advance <a href=""/wiki/Statistics"" title=""Statistics"">statistics</a>, although the term is sometimes restricted to label the use of algebraic geometry and <a href=""/wiki/Commutative_algebra"" title=""Commutative algebra"">commutative algebra</a> in <a href=""/wiki/Statistics"" title=""Statistics"">statistics</a>.
<b><a href=""/wiki/Algebraic_topology"" title=""Algebraic topology"">Algebraic topology</a></b>|a branch that uses tools from <a href=""/wiki/Abstract_algebra"" title=""Abstract algebra"">abstract algebra</a> for <a href=""/wiki/Topology"" title=""Topology"">topology</a> to study <a href=""/wiki/Topological_space"" title=""Topological space"">topological spaces</a>.
